# Ethics & ToS — Responsible Competitive Intelligence

Competitive intelligence done with public information is legitimate and standard
practice. Doing it carelessly creates legal, ethical, and reputational risk and
produces unreliable deliverables. These rules are non-negotiable for this suite.

## Allowed (public-source CI)

- Reading public web pages, pricing pages, blogs, docs, and marketing sites.
- Ad transparency tools: **Meta Ad Library**, **Google Ads Transparency Center**,
  **LinkedIn Ads** tab, **TikTok Creative Center** — these exist to be browsed.
- Public review sites (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Trustpilot, Gartner Peer Insights).
- Public social profiles and follower/engagement counts.
- The **Wayback Machine** for historical public pages.
- Press, filings, funding announcements, public analyst notes.
- Synthesizing all of the above into analysis.

## Not allowed

- **No impersonation.** Do not pose as a customer, prospect, journalist, or job
  applicant to extract non-public info. Do not create fake accounts.
- **No accessing gated/auth/paywalled data** you aren't entitled to — no logging
  into a competitor's product, customer portal, or private community.
- **No circumventing technical barriers** (auth walls, rate limits, paywalls,
  anti-bot). If a source requires login or blocks fetching, treat it as unavailable
  and note the blind spot.
- **No misrepresenting findings as fact** when they're inference. Estimates are
  labeled as estimates with their basis.
- **No disparagement in battlecards.** Reframe with evidence; never instruct reps
  to lie about or trash a competitor. Battlecards steelman the rival, then counter.
- **No private personal data** on individual employees beyond public professional
  profile facts relevant to the analysis.

## Citation discipline (quality, not just ethics)

- Every quantitative or factual claim carries a **source URL + capture date**.
- Distinguish **observed** (cited) from **inferred** (labeled estimate + basis)
  from **unknown** (stated as a blind spot).
- Prefer current captures; competitive facts decay. Re-verify before reuse in
  monitoring.

## If asked to cross a line

If a request would require impersonation, breaching auth, or fabricating data, do
not do it. Say plainly what's off-limits and offer the strongest **public-source**
alternative that answers the underlying question. The public surface is almost
always enough to win the analysis.
